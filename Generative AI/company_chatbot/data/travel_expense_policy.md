# Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Document ID:** POL-FIN-003  
**Version:** 3.0  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Department:** Finance  

## Purpose
This policy establishes guidelines for business travel authorization, expense management, and reimbursement procedures.

## Scope
Applies to all employees, contractors, and approved visitors traveling on company business.

## Policy Overview

Employees will be reimbursed for reasonable and necessary business expenses when traveling on authorized company business. All expenses must be documented and submitted within specified timeframes.

## Travel Authorization

### Pre-Approval Requirements

**Domestic Travel:**
- Manager approval required for trips over $500
- Submit Travel Authorization Form at least 5 business days before travel
- Include: destination, purpose, dates, estimated costs

**International Travel:**
- Vice President approval required
- Submit request at least 15 business days in advance
- Additional requirements: passport validity, visa needs, travel insurance

**Conference/Training:**
- Requires department head approval
- Include conference details and business justification
- Registration fees must be pre-approved

## Eligible Expenses

### Transportation

**Air Travel:**
- Economy class for flights under 5 hours
- Premium economy for flights 5+ hours
- Business class allowed only for flights over 10 hours with VP approval
- Book through company travel portal for best rates
- Direct flights preferred when cost difference is less than $100

**Ground Transportation:**
- Taxis/rideshare for airport transfers and meetings
- Rental cars when cost-effective or multiple destinations
- Choose mid-size or economy vehicles
- Decline rental car insurance (company policy covers)
- Personal vehicle: $0.67 per mile reimbursement

**Train/Bus:**
- Standard/coach class
- Business class allowed for trips over 3 hours

### Accommodation

**Hotel Standards:**
- Stay at reasonably-priced hotels (typically $150-250/night)
- Book through company travel portal when possible
- Extended stay accommodations for trips over 5 days
- Airbnb/VRBO allowed if comparable or lower cost

**Maximum Rates by City Tier:**
- Tier 1 (NYC, SF, LA): $275/night
- Tier 2 (Major cities): $200/night  
- Tier 3 (Other locations): $150/night

**Not Reimbursed:**
- In-room movies or entertainment
- Mini-bar charges
- Spa services
- Room service (except when no other option available)

### Meals and Incidentals

**Per Diem Rates:**
- Breakfast: $15
- Lunch: $20
- Dinner: $35
- Daily maximum: $70 (or $75 for high-cost cities)

**Meal Guidelines:**
- No receipts required for per diem
- Cannot claim per diem if meals provided at conference/event
- Alcoholic beverages: maximum $20/day, receipts required
- Coffee/snacks: included in per diem

**Business Meals:**
- Client dinners require receipt and attendees listed
- Maximum $100/person at high-end establishments
- Requires business purpose justification

### Other Expenses

**Communications:**
- International phone plan: up to $50/trip if traveling abroad
- Wi-Fi fees when hotel doesn't provide free access
- Printing/shipping of business materials

**Conference/Event:**
- Registration fees (pre-approved)
- Required materials or membership dues
- Parking at venue

**Not Covered:**
- Personal entertainment
- Laundry (unless trip exceeds 5 days)
- Gym fees or fitness classes
- Traffic violations or parking tickets
- Lost/stolen personal items

## Expense Submission Process

### Timeline
- Submit expenses within **10 business days** after trip completion
- Late submissions (over 30 days) may be denied
- Expenses over 90 days old will not be reimbursed

### Required Documentation

1. **Expense Report (ER-FORM-001):**
   - Complete all required fields
   - Itemize each expense by category
   - Provide business purpose for each item
   - Manager signature required

2. **Receipts:**
   - Original itemized receipts for all expenses over $25
   - Credit card statements not accepted
   - For lost receipts: completed Lost Receipt Affidavit
   - Digital photos/scans acceptable

3. **Supporting Documents:**
   - Travel authorization form
   - Conference/event agenda
   - Client meeting attendee list (for business meals)

### Submission Methods

**Online (Preferred):**
1. Log into expense portal: expenses.company.com
2. Upload receipts (PDF or image format)
3. Complete expense form
4. Submit for manager approval

**Paper Form:**
1. Complete ER-FORM-001
2. Attach all receipts to form
3. Obtain manager signature
4. Submit to Finance department (Mail Stop 45)

### Approval Workflow

1. **Manager Review:** 3 business days
   - Verifies business purpose
   - Checks compliance with policy
   - Approves or returns for correction

2. **Finance Review:** 2 business days
   - Validates receipt documentation
   - Verifies calculations
   - Checks coding and GL accounts

3. **Payment Processing:** 5-7 business days
   - Reimbursement via direct deposit
   - Payment on next biweekly payroll cycle

## Credit Card Use

### Company Credit Card
- Available to frequent travelers
- Apply through Finance department
- Monthly reconciliation required within 5 days of statement
- Personal use prohibited

### Personal Credit Card
- Can be used for business expenses
- Standard reimbursement process applies
- No responsibility for interest on personal cards

## Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following will **NOT** be reimbursed under any circumstances:
- Personal travel or vacation days added to business trip
- Companion or family member expenses
- Excessive or luxury expenses
- Expenses without receipts (over $25)
- Late fees or finance charges
- Travel insurance (company policy provides coverage)
- Commuting to regular workplace
- Membership dues (unless pre-approved)

## Special Circumstances

### Extended Business Travel (30+ days)
- May qualify for temporary housing allowance
- Contact HR for relocation assistance program
- Different tax treatment may apply

### International Travel
- Currency conversion at daily exchange rate
- Keep foreign receipts and note exchange rate used
- International per diem rates may differ (see Finance portal)

### Emergency Situations
- Use best judgment for safety and well-being
- Document circumstances in expense report
- Contact manager as soon as possible

## Exceptions and Waivers

Exceptions to this policy require:
- Written justification
- Senior leadership approval (VP or above)
- Submitted with expense report

## Audit and Compliance

- Finance conducts random expense audits
- Non-compliance may result in:
  - Reimbursement denial
  - Repayment requirement
  - Loss of travel privileges
  - Disciplinary action

## Tax Implications

- Properly documented business expenses are non-taxable
- Personal expenses or policy violations may be treated as taxable income
- Consult Finance with questions about tax treatment
