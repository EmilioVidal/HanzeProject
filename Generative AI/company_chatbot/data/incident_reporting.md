# Incident Reporting and Management Procedure

**Document ID:** PROC-HS-002  
**Version:** 1.3  
**Effective Date:** March 1, 2024  
**Department:** Health & Safety  

## Overview
This procedure outlines the process for reporting, investigating, and managing workplace incidents including accidents, near-misses, and safety hazards.

## When to Report an Incident

All employees must report:
- **Accidents:** Any event causing injury, illness, or property damage
- **Near-misses:** Events that could have caused injury but didn't
- **Unsafe conditions:** Hazards that could lead to incidents
- **Equipment failures:** Malfunctions that pose safety risks
- **Environmental spills:** Release of hazardous materials

## Reporting Timeline

| Incident Type | Reporting Deadline | Investigation Timeline |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Serious injury/emergency | Immediately | Within 24 hours |
| Minor injury | Same business day | Within 3 business days |
| Near-miss | Within 24 hours | Within 5 business days |
| Unsafe condition | Within 48 hours | Within 7 business days |

## Step-by-Step Reporting Process

### Step 1: Immediate Response
1. Ensure safety of all personnel
2. Call emergency services if needed (dial 911)
3. Provide first aid if qualified
4. Secure the incident scene (do not disturb evidence)
5. Notify your supervisor immediately

### Step 2: Initial Incident Report
1. Log into the company intranet
2. Navigate to "Safety & Compliance" â†’ "Incident Reporting"
3. Click "Report New Incident"
4. Complete the online form including:
   - Date, time, and location of incident
   - Description of what happened
   - People involved (names, departments)
   - Witnesses (names and contact info)
   - Immediate actions taken
   - Photos (if safe to take)

### Step 3: Supervisor Notification
Your supervisor will:
- Review the incident report within 2 hours
- Assess severity and determine investigation needs
- Notify Health & Safety department if required
- Implement immediate corrective actions
- Ensure injured person receives medical attention

### Step 4: Investigation
For significant incidents, the investigation team will:
1. Interview the affected person and witnesses
2. Examine the incident location
3. Review relevant procedures and training records
4. Identify root causes using 5-Why analysis
5. Document findings in investigation report

### Step 5: Corrective Actions
Based on investigation findings:
1. Identify preventive measures
2. Assign responsibility for each action
3. Set completion deadlines
4. Implement changes (training, equipment, procedures)
5. Communicate lessons learned to relevant teams

### Step 6: Follow-up
- Track corrective action completion
- Verify effectiveness of implemented changes
- Update risk assessments if needed
- Close incident report once all actions complete

## Severity Classification

**Level 1 - Critical:**
- Fatality or permanent disability
- Hospitalization required
- Major environmental damage
- Requires regulatory notification

**Level 2 - Serious:**
- Lost time injury (absence beyond incident day)
- Significant property damage (>$10,000)
- Near-miss with high potential severity

**Level 3 - Minor:**
- First aid treatment only
- No lost time
- Property damage <$10,000
- Low-risk near-miss

**Level 4 - Observation:**
- Unsafe condition identified
- No injury or damage
- Hazard that requires attention

## Confidentiality and Non-Retaliation

- All incident reports are confidential
- Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- No retaliation for reporting incidents in good faith
- Identity of reporters protected where possible
- Focus is on prevention, not blame

## Recordkeeping

- All incidents recorded in safety database
- Records retained for minimum 7 years
- Monthly incident summary reports generated
- Trends analyzed quarterly by safety committee
- Annual safety performance review

All employees must complete:
- New hire safety orientation
- Annual incident reporting refresher
- Role-specific safety training

Supervisors require additional training on:
- Incident investigation techniques
- Root cause analysis
- Corrective action planning

## Key Definitions

**Incident:** Any unplanned event that causes or could cause injury, illness, property damage, or environmental harm.

**Near-miss:** An unplanned event that did not result in injury, illness, or damage, but had the potential to do so.

**Root Cause:** The underlying reason an incident occurred, not just the immediate cause.

**Corrective Action:** Steps taken to eliminate the cause of an incident and prevent recurrence.

**Lost Time Injury (LTI):** Injury resulting in inability to work the next scheduled shift.
